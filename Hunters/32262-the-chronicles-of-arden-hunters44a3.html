      One moment Ling is admiring Alaris' wings with Shivala, thinking "Yes, we've got this!". Then everything falls apart.

      The fleder jumps and grabs Alaris mid-air. They fight, the creature tries to bite into them, but Alaris looks like they're getting the upper hand. The second fleder scurries toward them, straight up the wall, but with the three of them on the roof, Ling still thinks they've got everything under control. That is, until the massive, snarling fleder appears through the tower's archway. It dives into the church and goes straight for the dwarf, knocking her halfway across the church into the pews with a loud thud. Astrid isn't moving, but their enemy has not had enough. Ling watches in horror as the monster picks up the small woman and bites into her. Anger churns in her stomach and she clenches her fist. 

      "Ling, get me down there, now!" Nikolai shouts next to her. Not needing to be told twice, she turns and pulls him onto her broom, hardly losing speed as she whips past. Through the roof and down into the church they go, and as they descend, Ling holds on to Nik with one hand while reaching out with the other, her eyes darkening all over. Both fleder suddenly emit high pitched shrieks as shadows twirl around their heads and with another snap of her fingers, these tendrils zap into their ears, deafening them. "Drop me!" Nik yells over the screeching and without hesitation, Ling pushes him down. She extends her hand after him as if to catch him and his fall slows until he softly lands right next to Astrid. Even though she obeyed him without question, dropping Nikolai into the middle of battle feels like tearing out her insides and offering them up to the elements. No, he's got this - Alaris? She spins around, her white hair a flash of light. Alaris and the fleder are a tangle of black and white wings, turning in the air. Being unable to help them any further and trusting that Nik will get Astrid out of there, Ling speeds back up and wipes the sweat of her forehead, breathing hard. She takes a moment to focus on thoughts of clear, open night skies again, making sure to keep the other three creatures entranced. When she resurfaces, she shares a look with Shiva that says: It's hell down there.

      Nikolai smiles as Ling follows his lead without questioning him. A brief thought hits him just as she pushes him. 'Good girl, she's come so far from when we took her in...' And then the shrieking and screaming recalls his attention as his feet lightly touch down on the ground next to the dwarf locked in a death grip with the massive fleder. She looked on the verge of death, pale and bleeding everywhere... he just hoped he wasn't too late! Nikolai locks eyes with the fleder and stares the monster down as he wraps his arms around Astrid's waist. "Peace, Astrid, it's Nikolai. I've got you!" His eyes never leave the fleder's as a dark aura begins to build around Nikolai and Astrid and he grins, showing his canine teeth in a predatorial smile as the dark aura streaks between them into the chest of the fleder which begins to swirl like a vortex. The fleder's eyes widen as Nikolai and Astrid feel an abrupt gravitational shift as the vortex draws them inward... "She's mine, beast." Nikolai whispers as the two of them are sucked into the vortex with a cacophonous explosion of sound that rattles the sensitive hearing of the creature a searing burn sizzles in its chest as the vortex flares with a bright white light as it collapses in on its self...

      At that same moment Nikolai and Astrid are standing safely atop the roof. Nikolai looks at the severe wound that is rapidly draining Astrid of her life force with worried eyes. He did all he could, there was nothing more he could do. "Astrid, you must heal yourself! Call upon your goddess."

           Astrid watches as the fleder flee up to the rafters. She beams confidently at the group’s early success and turns back to Caio. “Well, looks like it’s just the two of us here to hold the ground floor.” She smirks. “You got some wings you're hiding too?” She nods her head back to the paladin that just sprouted a pair of wings and took to the air.

            Then, a chill runs down her spine and her keen perception detects a large shadow looming overhead. Boards crack and splinters fly. The impact alone is almost enough to knock the cleric to her feet, and Astrid turns to find herself toe-to-toe with another one of the elder fleder creatures. “Scribe, protect me!” Her smirk fades and she grit her teeth. She easily ducks to avoid the first claw of the monster and then pulls her shield in close trying to brace for the claw from the other side. Her attempt is fruitless. The dwarf is unable to withstand the powerful blow of the monstrous bat and she is knocked to the ground – with a thud, her head makes contact. A cut opens up over her eye and everything becomes a blur to her. The giant fleder scoops up the dazed dwarf and, being infused with the desires of vampires, sinks its fangs into Astrid’s neck. The sharp pain momentarily breaks through her stupor from the blow to her head.

            Time slows down. Her eyes go wide, staring far into the distance. Images from the recent days come to her when she was saying her goodbyes to her parents just before she set off from Kazad Azgaraz. Her mother, Nelstrid Silverscar, was so hopeful to get answers as to her own mother’s disappearance and that honor would soon be restored to the clan. Now, as she feels her life being drained by this abomination, all she feels is disappointment and failure.

            The chaos from within the room causes her to return to the present, though still in a haze having half her life drained. Through watery eyes, she sees a bluish figure descending like an angel. "Peace… I've got you!"  She finds herself unable to form any words, only producing incoherent sounds as she reaches out to this savior from Brightmantle. A portal appears next to them, to the dwarf like a book opening up before her. With a thunderous crash, she feels weightlessness as they pass through the threshold.... then, fresh air.

      The massive fleder hits the room like a hurricane, and Caio is already reaching for his sword and shifting the Raven Queen’s hungry ire to the behemoth as all hell begins to break loose.

      The severity of Lings eyes tell of the dire shift in combat. Taking several steps forward, Shiva sees the giant fleder, Caio rising to meet it, Alaris entangled with one of the creatures, and Nik cradling a horribly wounded Astrid. The veins along her arms and neck visibly rise beneath the skin as her heart pounds, turning her blood into fire as her rage urges her forward.

      Caio is a black clad arrow as he launches towards his quarry. His fury is ice cold, his face a stoic mask. Within moments he is in range, dashing through the shadows left in the wake of Nik’s magic even as they cling to his form. Suddenly a foot plants into the ground and his momentum shifts upwards at the grotesque visage dripping with his newfound friend’s blood.

      As she sees Caio moving into range with the enormous ceature, Shiva breaks into a full sprint. Charging across the roof, she makes towards a large hole near its center. With three long bounds, she launches herself up, then dives through the opening towards the melee. She doesn't even hear the wind whistling in her ear as she plummets towards the ground, such is the intensity of her rage-fueled focus on her target.

      Silver rings against silver as their blades meet. Caio’s sword soars skyward just as Shiva’s sword crashes down like a tidal wave. The brilliant arcs both find the fleder’s face at their zeniths. Its grimace is nearly split in two, and a ruinous scream bursts out of the crimson wreckage. The creature can barely comprehend the double sided assault, and still the warriors continue their momentum: Shiva continues her infernal descent as Caio spins and sends the blade back around for another strike.

      When she is no more than just 30 feet above the unyielding floor of the church, Shiva strikes out with her blade. A metallic clang resonates through the air as her weapon momentarily meets that of Caio, before plunging into the fleder's armpit. The hunter then turns on his heel in a flash of movement to deliver another lethal blow to the abdomen, as Shiva throws the momentum of her fall, tearing downward along the side of the beast and dramatically slowing her descent. Planting her feet on solid ground, she rips the sword free, sending steaming viscera splattering out of the open wound. With Caio now at her side, she straightens to attack again.
