            Astrid stares wide-eyed as Ling bursts into laughter and lets out her own little nervous laugh. When the tiefling steps away to watch the others work on the door, she remains in leaning against the sarcophagus staring far off mumbling inaudibly, rubbing her temple with two fingers. She again briefly questions her faith in wondering why her patron brought her this one to continually test her patients. 

           The dwarf gratefully breathes in the fresher air once the group reemerges to the street level. She follows along quietly back towards the church. "It can't do anything like that as far as I know," she says when Alaris returns from scouting.

      Nikolai blasts roof repeatedly, easily splintering the smaller support beams and making several large sections crash down into the church. The fleder below screech and scatter, gliding to the floor in confusion. They try to avoid the light, huddling in the shadier areas down below. From what Ling can see, there seem to be at least ten of them. He has her drop him off on one of the large, sturdy wooden beams that run across the middle of the roof, then Ling hovers back down and relays his plan to the others.

      "So the three of us will shoot at them from above, Nik from the roof," she points to where he is standing, "and Shiva and I will be mobile. Then Caio, Alaris and shorty can go through the door and attack from there, use it as a choke point maybe, whatever works. Anyone have any questions? Okay, good, because I couldn't answer them anyway. Let's get slaying!" Ling hops back on, waiting for Shiva to get on the broom with her, and then flies back up and hovers over one of the larger holes in the roof, carefully surveying the area below. One of her hands holds onto the broom, the other begins gathering shadow between her fingers. "Hold on tight, this is going to be a bumpy ride - just the way you like it!" Ling laughs before diving in.

      As soon as they enter the church, Ling lets go of her broom, holding on tight with her legs and pulls her hands apart to create a swirling orb of shadow. She pushes it forward and it surges towards the creatures, momentarily wrapping them in darkness. It dissipates almost immediately, but the creatures eyes remain dark and they stop moving, looking dazed. Sweet dreams, little bats.

           "Shorty" glances sideways as Ling rattles off the plan excitedly. She approaches the door with the others. Pressing her ear up to the door, the perceptive dwarf hears the laughs of the pair as they swoop in and glances over to the other two saying on the ground with her. "Are we ready do this?" she asks, trying to persuade herself. 

           Pushing open the door, she takes a couple of steps in and immediately sees the three fleder entranced in a swirling haze at the far end of the worship area.  From each side, in the shadows near the windows she sees another pair and halts her advance. The cleric turns back to Alaris and gives them a meek smile, "Maybe it'll be better if you take the lead." Grabbing the watch around her neck in one hand, she rapidly pushes the hands around the face clockwise with her thumb while holding her palm out towards the paladin. "A little something to get the jump on them."

      As Ling and Shiva drift down through the roof of the temple, several things about its layout become apparent to them.

      At the rear of the temple, behind the altar, sits a traditional half-domed alcove containing shrines to several gods. The alcove is twenty-five feet high, and above it a faded fresco depicting an eagle sitting atop a might oak rises another twenty-five feet to the eaves. The apex of the roof is a total of seventy-five feet off the ground, and the fresco itself is flanked by doors that open onto balconies overlooking the pews. Each balcony runs forty feet along the length of the temple and is supported by thick stone pillars, spaced every ten feet. At the far end, another door opens into the stone wall of the bell tower, which runs the height of the temple. In the centre of the tower's first floor an arch-shaped window overlooks the interior, and on the ground floor a larger archway extends all the way through the bottom of the bell tower to the temple doors. The opening is twenty feet wide and fifteen feet deep, and two smaller doors sit against the sides of the temple, leading inside the feet of the tower. These are mirrored by two other ground level doors either side of the alcove at the back of the building.

           Having cast her spell, Astrid turn back in to survey the interior of the church. "Watch out for anything that may be hiding up there," she calls to the pair on the broom while pointing up to the balconies on either side.

           Caio swiftly follows behind the dwarf, shrugging the longbow from his back and nocking an arrow. He takes up position at the edge of the entryway and draws his bow taking aim at the fleder lurking in the shadows to his right. The ranger focuses his concentration on his target looking for any weakness or opportunity that the creature may present. He holds his breath and releases the arrow, immediately readying another arrow and firing again. He looks pleased as both missiles find their mark in the upper thigh of the fleder.

      In the midst of the temple, the three fleder entranced by Ling's enchantment stand stock still in the sunlight, gazing into the swirling pattern of lights. The remaining two monsters hug the walls, deftly skittering up the sides to the balconies on the second level.

           Having not paid any attention behind her and not noticing the door to the side of the entrance, Astrid is fortunate. She is oblivious to the to the first strike that sails overhead, but then emits a sharp squeal when the claws rake across her backside beneath her breastplate, causing a spray of blood across the stones. Her pulse quickens realizing the danger she is now in. The feeling abates somewhat when the paladin delivers their healing touch. "Ouch... That's sure to leave a mark! Well, uh, thank you!"

           The dwarf staggers as she turns to face the creature standing in the doorway. Glaring at the creature, she pulls up her sleeve and reaches up into the air above her head. With a flourish of her wrist, she pulls a glowing shard as if from no where and then lunges at the creature. However, her strike is easily deflected away. Clearly shaken, she carefully steps away from her attacker, summons her spiritual weapon and focuses on the creature lying at Alaris's feet. The buzzsaw-like clockwork gears grind into the creature and the cleric stands up a little more straight, apparently pleased with her effort.

      As the action below unfolds, Shiva reaches out once more, releasing two more blasts of golden energy from her blade. But this time, out of the corner of her eye, she notices Ling's figure as she leans forward on Sybaris. Having spent so much time with her, she rarely even picks up on it anymore; the way in which the curves of her body gently flow into one another. Combined with her natural charm, wit and allure, it's a wonder that anyone is able to resist her. So taken by the momentary observation is Shiva that her aim drifts ever so slightly off-mark. Yet again, the attacks fail in their purpose, with one blowing a hole through a nearby wall. The other finds its target, but the fleder is merely knocked off balance, before resuming it's attacks.

      Snapping back to reality, Shiva watches on as the battle becomes more intense and becomes frustrated with herself "I don't think I'm doing much good up here. I need to get down there."
