The sensations of the beast fade as Silas slips into unconsciousness. Lying there in torpor it feels as if he is floating in an empty void, unaware of the world around him. He feels a bit weaker in the void, almost anemic. It feels as tho hours have passed in his mind, when the void shifts into something more, something familiar. There standing in his ripped and bloodied suit and tie, Silas stands on the familiar battlefields of colonial America as he sees the barren field filled with dead soldiers, some faces he recognized. He walks through the field until he reaches the campsite base he stayed at when he fought in the war. He enters the medical tent and sees one of his comrades, he remembers him as Matthew a young boy who recently joined the battle and looked at Silas like his senior. His arm was being sawed off as the gunshot wound on his bicep would soon cause an infection. No anesthesia, no mercy, just a cloth in his mouth to clench on to and to hold back his screams of agony. Silas watches intensely, he can't look away, yet he feels nothing, no sadness for the boy he once knew suffering in front of him. He continues to watch the surgery in his own void.
