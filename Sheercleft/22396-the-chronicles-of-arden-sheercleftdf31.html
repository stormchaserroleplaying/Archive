      The Norscan holds Gabe's look. 

      "And I do not appreciate to be patronize by fiends. Godlike or not. Oh I am being polite. At least trying to be as what you civilized people call polite. But I was there. I had a talk to Vark. He wasn't the master of his own body. He wasn't aware of what he was doing. I am sure that you offered him a deal in that moment, but I am sure too that you use those deals in your own benefit. Look, I love the boy, and I respect him. In the name of that love and respect I am going to give you a vote of confidence. But if I smell the smallest treachery in your deals with him... it would probably end badly. " outside a distant thunder can be heard, just a rumor in the room they are gathered. "Now, let's put that behind us and focus in our current problem. We should be sure that there is still a Sheercleft when Vark returns. So this Marcellus is just a mad dog who has been released of its leash, hiring gnolls with the excuse that we are beneath himself. An honorable man conducts himself with honor no matter the status or his enemies, therefore he is no an honorable man, I do not care his titles or his status. I am concern about the people of Sheercleft and his troops, mercenary or regular. I am not afraid of him, if he want to fight us he will face the mighty wrath of the Archanost. But I do suspect he will not dare to face all of us at the same time. We should talk to Jex and start sleeping in the same room making turns as we do in the wild. He could send assasins in an attempt to diminish our numbers before they get here, and to crush the moral of our people. We also should contact the dwarves " he says mostly to Seid and Bründir "and make that naming you Lord oficial as fast as possible..."

      Aiden notes the slight red glow around the whites of Gabe's eyes -- the same red that bled from Vark's magic, whenever he called on Matthew's power. Aiden quirks an eyebrow at the display. Surely it was unintentional. Surely Matthew didn't feel the need to make a show of his power to intimidate them -- not someone as sure of themselves as he. Either Matthew felt quite fanatical about his deals and the red glow was a reflection of that, or Thurston had touched a nerve with the fiend. Either way, the detail was interesting. 

      "Gabe," Aiden says, emphasizing the pseudonym for its obvious falseness. "You seem highly concerned with deals and contracts and the like. You are not making a contract with us right now, are you? Surely your assistance now comes as an extension of Vark's contract with you." 

      It was a fair question, in his mind. And it would poke Matthew's fanaticism or sensitivity about the issue.

      It had been 11 days since Nakia had arrived in Sheercleft. No sooner had she arrived than Jex was gone, one brief night, a hushed, rushed conversation and a letter and he had gone with the sun. That had always been his way. He had done well though, that she could not deny. He had completed his task and more and what he was building here could prove incredibly fruitful. She glances over the paperwork once again, running a hand through the blackened ends of hair that still clung to her head. He was clever, for sure, everything worked and the projections were good, but some of it was just not sensible, not efficient.

      “Thank your stars that I am here so soon Jex, This could have cost a small fortune.”

       Downstairs the volunteers were beginning to clang the pots and pans as the orphanage came to life. It had taken her little time to become a part of this family, even with no introduction from Jex, they seemed happy to take her word that she spoke for him, and she had enjoyed the days working alongside them. The schooling had needed looking at, Jex’s idea of education was rather different from hers and some of the lessons the children were learning seemed inappropriate and the teachers inadequate. Something she could soon fix though, much of her retinue was back in Quenlan, but soon they would begin to arrive. There was a tap on the door and a small boy poked his head inside. She greeted him with a wide smile.

      “Good morning Barnaby, what news.”

      The small boy shuffled his feet.

      “He has not arrived, the kobolds watched all night. There is urm…”

      Nakia smiles again warmly, inviting him to continue.

      “You asked to hear about the tower, they all seem to be gathering, and we, we heard another voice, but we couldn’t get any closer. Maybe he snuck back in?”

      Nakia nods and hands the boy a coin. “Thank you, most helpful. I will be late for breakfast, please save me some oats and warmed water, but do not wait for me.”

      She pats Barnaby on the head as she leaves. As ever, her footsteps fall evenly, and from a distance even Jex would not know that she hurried. There was, to the canniest observer though, a slight pace to her steps as she stopped around the corner in the chapel to light the flame at the small alter behind the false wall, and to give her prayers to the deity whose likeness stared down at her from the stone. Extinguishing the flame as her prayers finish, she scatters the ash across the alter and pulls her cloak tight around her as she once again hurries across the square to the foot of the tower. There is no grace to her movement and her face is common and few would describe her as pretty, but the kindness in her eyes and soft tunes she often sung had few feeling anything but positive thoughts of her and she was well known and liked amongst the tavern dwellers and miners in the village despite her short time here. Nobody stopped or questioned as she entered the tower and climbed the staircase, hoping to listen at the door she finds it open though. Caught in an awkward situation, she considers turning around but worries it would look too strange. She instead taps, almost nervously to announce her presence.

      “Excuse me, I saw you all arriving from across the way there, and just wondered if I might ask a question.”

      Her eyes dart towards Gabe curiously, but she forces her attention back to those she has seen from the meetings and the blot.
